Concerning Law on Foreign Affairs, at the initiative of the Ministry of Justice and Public Affairs. According to the existing provision an extradition could be refused if prosecution or execution of the sentence was barred by the law of the requested or appealing state, which could result in the person not being held responsible, under the new protocol limitation of the law enforcement no longer a sufficient reason. The Protocol also regulates a different arrangement of the central authorities. Countries will no longer need to send original documents. The main change is the shortening of deadlines, elaborated prohibitions and restrictions and partial removal of the restrictions. It aims to shorten the deadline for leaving the country, it allows for procedural steps in the requesting state if they are not of intrusive nature, and allows for detention prior to giving consent for prosecution under certain circumstances.
